**Global and U.S. EV Market Dynamics:**
The global electric vehicle market has experienced substantial growth. In 2015, worldwide EV sales were around 500,000 units. Forecasts indicate a forty-fold increase, reaching an estimated 20 million units sold worldwide by 2025 [5]. The U.S. market mirrors this trend, with EVs accounting for 0.70% of the market in 2015 and expanding to 10% of new car sales by 2024. **Battery Cost as a Driver of Adoption:**
A significant factor contributing to the mainstream adoption of EVs is the reduction in the cost of lithium-ion battery packs. Batteries typically represent 30% to 50% of an EV's total cost. Globally, pack prices have dramatically decreased from nearly $1,400 per kWh in 2010 to $115 per kWh in 2024, a 20% year-on-year decline [5]. This cost reduction has been instrumental in transitioning EVs from a niche product to a more accessible option.
